Matcha contains about **38-88mg of caffeine per teaspoon**, depending on leaf quality, growing conditions, and how densely you scoop the powder. That makes it stronger than most brewed green tea, usually gentler than brewed coffee, and surprisingly close to espresso when prepared as a concentrated latte.

Matcha can feel different because you drink the whole powdered leaf, not just an infusion. You get caffeine alongside L-theanine, a tea amino acid that can make the rise feel smoother and less abrupt.

## Matcha vs. green tea, coffee, and espresso

| Drink | Typical serving | Caffeine |
|---|---:|---:|
| Ceremonial matcha | 1 tsp powder | 60-88mg |
| Culinary matcha | 1 tsp powder | 38-70mg |
| Brewed green tea | 8oz | 25-45mg |
| Black tea | 8oz | 40-70mg |
| Brewed coffee | 8oz | 80-120mg |
| Espresso | 1 shot | 60-75mg |
| Cold brew | 12oz | 150-240mg |

The biggest source of variation is serving size. A cafe matcha latte may use two teaspoons, which can put one drink near 120-160mg before you realise it.

## Why matcha caffeine feels smoother

L-theanine does not cancel caffeine, but it can change the experience. Many people describe matcha as calmer than coffee because the stimulant effect arrives with a more relaxed alertness.

That does not mean matcha is sleep-safe. If you drink enough of it late in the day, the same half-life rules apply: after about five hours, roughly half the caffeine is still active.

## How much matcha is too much?

For most healthy adults, 400mg per day is the common upper guideline. If your matcha is around 70mg per teaspoon, that is about five teaspoons across the day, before counting coffee, tea, cola, or chocolate.

Pregnant or breastfeeding people are usually advised to stay closer to 200mg per day. Two strong matcha lattes can reach that quickly, so measure the powder rather than guessing.

## Matcha and sleep

If bedtime is 11pm and your matcha latte contains 140mg of caffeine, a practical cut-off is early afternoon. The goal is not zero caffeine at bedtime; it is getting below the level that tends to disturb deep sleep.

## Ceremonial vs. culinary grade

Grade does not guarantee caffeine content. Ceremonial matcha often uses younger shaded leaves, which can be higher in caffeine, but scoop size and recipe strength matter more for your actual dose.

## FAQ

### Does matcha have more caffeine than coffee?

Per teaspoon, matcha usually has less caffeine than an 8oz coffee. Per latte, it depends how much powder the cafe uses.

### Does matcha cause jitters?

It can. L-theanine may smooth the feeling, but high doses, low sleep, and dehydration still raise the chance of jitters.

### How much matcha per day is reasonable?

One to three teaspoons is a common range for healthy adults, depending on your total caffeine from other sources.

### Is matcha safe during pregnancy?

Many people can include small amounts, but keep total caffeine near the recommended pregnancy limit and follow your clinician's advice.
