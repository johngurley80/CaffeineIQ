## Quick Answer

**Jasmine tea contains approximately 20–45mg of caffeine per 8oz cup.** The caffeine comes from the base tea (usually green tea or sometimes white tea) — the jasmine flowers themselves contain no caffeine.

---

## Why Jasmine Tea Has Caffeine (But Not Much)

Jasmine tea is made by scenting tea leaves with jasmine blossoms during processing. The flowers impart fragrance and flavour, but they contribute zero caffeine. The caffeine is entirely from the base tea.

Most commercial jasmine tea uses **green tea** as the base. Green tea is lower in caffeine than black tea, which is why jasmine tea tends to sit at the gentler end of the caffeine spectrum.

Some premium jasmine teas use **white tea** as the base, which is lower still (15–30mg per 8oz).

## Jasmine Tea Caffeine vs Other Teas

| Tea | Caffeine per 8oz |
|---|---|
| Assam black tea | 60–70mg |
| English Breakfast tea | 40–60mg |
| Jasmine tea (green tea base) | 20–45mg |
| Standard green tea | 20–45mg |
| Jasmine tea (white tea base) | 15–30mg |
| White tea | 15–30mg |
| Chamomile (herbal) | 0mg |
| Rooibos (herbal) | 0mg |

Jasmine green tea and standard green tea are broadly equivalent in caffeine, since the jasmine scenting process doesn't change the caffeine level of the base leaf.

---

## Is Jasmine Tea Low in Caffeine?

Relative to coffee (80–100mg) and black tea (40–70mg), yes — jasmine tea is on the lower end. However, it's not caffeine-free, and multiple cups add up.

Three cups of jasmine tea over the course of a day delivers 60–135mg of caffeine — still within healthy limits for most adults, but worth tracking if you're caffeine-sensitive or pregnant (200mg/day limit).

---

## Can I Drink Jasmine Tea in the Evening?

Jasmine tea's lower caffeine content (20–45mg) makes it one of the more sleep-friendly caffeinated teas. For most people, a single cup in the early evening (5–6pm with a 10–11pm bedtime) is unlikely to cause significant sleep disruption.

But "lower caffeine" isn't the same as "no caffeine". The 5-hour half-life still applies. If you have high caffeine sensitivity, even 20–30mg in the evening can delay sleep onset.

If you're looking for a genuinely calming evening drink with the ritual feel of tea:

- **Rooibos** — 0mg, naturally sweet, South African red tea
- **Chamomile** — 0mg, mildly sedating, classic sleep tea
- **Peppermint** — 0mg, refreshing with no caffeine
- **Jasmine herbal blend** — 0mg if made with jasmine + rooibos rather than green tea base

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does jasmine tea have caffeine?
Yes. Jasmine tea contains approximately 20–45mg of caffeine per 8oz cup. The caffeine comes from the base tea (usually green tea) — the jasmine flowers add fragrance but no caffeine.

### Is jasmine tea lower in caffeine than black tea?
Yes, significantly. Jasmine tea (green tea base) contains 20–45mg per 8oz, compared to 40–70mg for black tea. It's roughly half the caffeine.

### Can jasmine tea keep you awake?
For most people, one cup of jasmine tea has too little caffeine to significantly disrupt sleep if consumed a few hours before bed. However, caffeine-sensitive individuals or those with very late bedtimes may want to stop earlier.

### Is jasmine tea good for sleep?
Jasmine tea is not a sleep aid — it contains caffeine. For sleep-supporting evening drinks, herbal teas (chamomile, valerian, rooibos) are better choices. Jasmine tea is among the lower-caffeine options if you want the taste of tea in the evening.

### How much caffeine is in jasmine tea bags vs loose leaf?
The difference is minor. Both jasmine tea bags and loose leaf jasmine tea deliver approximately 20–45mg per 8oz cup, with variation based on brew strength and steep time rather than format.
