## Quick Answer

**An 8oz cup of brewed black tea contains 40–70mg of caffeine. An 8oz brewed coffee contains 80–100mg.** Black tea has roughly half the caffeine of coffee, though the gap narrows with a strong, long steep.

---

## Black Tea vs Coffee: Caffeine Numbers

| Drink | Caffeine per 8oz |
|---|---|
| Brewed coffee (drip) | 80–100mg |
| Espresso (1oz shot) | 63mg |
| Cold brew | 100–200mg |
| Assam black tea (strong, 5 min) | 60–70mg |
| Yorkshire Tea (standard bag, 3 min) | 45–55mg |
| PG Tips (standard bag, 3 min) | 40–50mg |
| English Breakfast tea (3 min) | 40–60mg |
| Earl Grey (3 min) | 40–60mg |
| Instant coffee | 60–80mg |

The type of black tea matters significantly. **Assam** is the highest-caffeine variety — it's the base of most builder's tea and strong breakfast blends. **Darjeeling** is lower. Steeping time is the biggest variable you can control: an extra 2 minutes can add 10–15mg.

---

## What Affects Caffeine in Black Tea?

**Steep time** — the single biggest factor. 1-minute steep: ~20mg. 5-minute steep: ~60mg.

**Tea type** — Assam > English Breakfast > Earl Grey > Darjeeling in terms of caffeine.

**Loose leaf vs tea bag** — broadly similar, but loose leaf is slightly more variable.

**Water temperature** — boiling water extracts caffeine faster. Below 90°C slows extraction.

**Cup size** — larger mugs mean more tea but also more water. If you use one bag in a large mug, the caffeine is the same — just more diluted.

## Does a Cup of Tea Keep You Awake?

Yes, if consumed too close to bedtime. At 40–70mg, black tea is a meaningful caffeine dose. The half-life is the same as coffee — approximately 5 hours. A cup of tea at 5pm still leaves 20–35mg in your system at 10pm.

The "tea is fine before bed" assumption is particularly common in the UK, where tea culture normalises late-evening cups. For people with caffeine sensitivity, a 9pm builders' tea can genuinely affect sleep quality.

## Switching from Coffee to Tea to Reduce Caffeine

If you're cutting back on caffeine by switching from coffee to black tea:

- You'll typically reduce from ~90mg to ~50mg per cup — a meaningful reduction
- Switch to green tea for a further reduction to 20–45mg
- Switch to herbal tea for complete elimination
- Be aware that strong black tea (particularly Assam, steeped 5+ minutes) can approach 70mg — closer to coffee than you might expect

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does black tea have caffeine?
Yes. Black tea contains 40–70mg of caffeine per 8oz cup depending on brewing time, tea variety, and bag strength. It's lower than coffee but a meaningful caffeine source.

### Which black tea has the most caffeine?
Assam tea is the highest-caffeine black tea variety, typically producing 60–70mg per 8oz with a standard steep. It's the base of most English Breakfast and builder's tea blends.

### Can black tea disrupt sleep?
Yes, if consumed within 5–6 hours of bedtime for most people. The caffeine in black tea has the same 5-hour half-life as coffee. A common UK habit of drinking tea in the evening is a genuine source of sleep disruption for caffeine-sensitive people.

### How does Yorkshire Tea compare to coffee in caffeine?
A standard Yorkshire Tea bag steeped for 3 minutes delivers approximately 45–55mg of caffeine per 8oz cup. A brewed coffee delivers 80–100mg — roughly twice as much.

### How can I reduce the caffeine in my black tea?
Steep for a shorter time (1–2 minutes removes around 30–50% less caffeine), use cooler water, or switch to a lower-caffeine variety like Darjeeling. Cold-steeping black tea in the fridge overnight also produces lower caffeine.
