No, **almost all ginger ale is caffeine-free**. Canada Dry, Schweppes, Seagram's, Vernors, Reed's, Bundaberg, and Fever-Tree are generally sold without caffeine.

The energy people feel from ginger ale usually comes from sugar, carbonation, and hydration rather than caffeine. Always check the label if you are buying a limited edition or mixed soda product.

## What's actually in ginger ale that gives you energy?

Regular ginger ale can contain a meaningful amount of sugar. That can create a short lift, especially if you have not eaten much, but it is not the same as caffeine stimulation.

Ginger compounds may also feel warming or settling, particularly when your stomach is off. The biggest practical benefit, though, is often fluid.

## Ginger ale vs. ginger beer

Ginger beer is usually caffeine-free too. The difference is flavour intensity and production style, not caffeine.

Some ginger beers are much higher in sugar, so compare labels if you are using them as a daily coffee replacement.

## When ginger ale is not a healthy choice

Ginger ale is caffeine-free, but it is not automatically a health drink. If one can adds 30-40g of sugar, it can leave you thirstier and less steady later.
