## Quick Answer

**Coffee has significantly more caffeine than most teas.** An 8oz brewed coffee contains 80–100mg; standard black tea contains 40–70mg. However, some teas — particularly strong matcha and certain chai lattes — can get close to coffee-level caffeine.

## The Complete Coffee vs Tea Caffeine Comparison

| Drink | Caffeine per 8oz |
|---|---|
| Brewed coffee (drip) | 80–100mg |
| Espresso (per 1oz shot) | 63mg |
| Cold brew coffee | 100–200mg |
| Black tea (brewed 3–5 min) | 40–70mg |
| Matcha (ceremonial, whisked) | 60–70mg |
| Oolong tea | 30–50mg |
| Green tea | 20–45mg |
| Chai tea latte (coffee shop) | 70–95mg |
| White tea | 15–30mg |
| Herbal tea (chamomile, peppermint, rooibos) | 0mg |
| Decaf coffee | 2–15mg |

The biggest caveat: **cold brew coffee** can contain up to 200mg per 8oz — easily double a standard brewed coffee and significantly more than any tea.

---

## Why Does Coffee Have More Caffeine?

Coffee beans contain more caffeine per gram than tea leaves. Additionally, a typical cup of coffee uses more plant material per serving than a standard tea bag. When you steep tea, you're also diluting the caffeine in a larger volume of water, whereas espresso is concentrated.

The exception is matcha — because you consume the entire ground leaf rather than steeping and discarding it, the caffeine extraction is much more complete than for other teas.

## Which Teas Have the Most Caffeine?

From highest to lowest:

1. **Matcha** — 60–70mg per 8oz (whole leaf consumed)
2. **Chai tea latte (coffee shop)** — 70–95mg (concentrate-based)
3. **Black tea (Assam, strong brew)** — up to 70mg
4. **Oolong tea** — 30–50mg
5. **Green tea** — 20–45mg
6. **White tea** — 15–30mg
7. **Herbal teas** — 0mg

## Does the Type of Tea Matter for Sleep?

Yes. The difference between 20mg (green tea) and 70mg (strong black tea) is significant if you're drinking in the afternoon. Both have the same 5-hour caffeine half-life, but the higher-caffeine tea will still have more caffeine in your system at bedtime.

## What About Caffeine Sensitivity?

Some people are genetically faster or slower caffeine metabolisers. For slow metabolisers, even 40mg from a cup of black tea at 4pm can be enough to disrupt sleep at 11pm. Your personal cut-off time depends on your weight, metabolism, and sensitivity — not just the mg count.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does coffee always have more caffeine than tea?
Almost always, yes. Brewed coffee (80–100mg) has more caffeine than most teas. The exceptions are coffee shop chai lattes (70–95mg) and strong matcha (60–70mg), which can get close.

### Which tea has the most caffeine?
Matcha is the highest-caffeine tea at 60–70mg per 8oz, because the whole ground leaf is consumed rather than steeped and discarded. Strong brewed black tea (Assam) can also reach 70mg.

### Does herbal tea have caffeine?
No. Herbal teas made from chamomile, peppermint, rooibos, hibiscus, or other plants contain zero caffeine. Only teas made from the Camellia sinensis plant (black, green, white, oolong, matcha) contain caffeine.

### Is tea better than coffee for reducing caffeine intake?
Generally yes — switching from brewed coffee (80–100mg) to black tea (40–70mg) cuts your caffeine roughly in half. Green tea (20–45mg) cuts it by 60–75%. Herbal tea eliminates it entirely.

### How does cold brew compare to tea for caffeine?
Cold brew coffee typically contains 100–200mg per 8oz — far higher than any tea. If you're watching your caffeine intake, cold brew is the drink to be most careful with.
