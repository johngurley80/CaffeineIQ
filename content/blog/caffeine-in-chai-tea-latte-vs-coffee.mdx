## Quick Answer

**A Starbucks Chai Tea Latte (grande) contains 95mg of caffeine — about the same as a medium brewed coffee.** Home-brewed chai lattes are considerably lower (40–60mg). The gap depends on how much concentrate is used.

---

## The Misconception That Chai Is Always Low-Caffeine

Many people switch from coffee to chai lattes thinking they're making a lower-caffeine choice. In a coffee shop, that's not always true.

Coffee shop chai lattes are made from **chai concentrate** — a pre-made, sweetened tea syrup. The concentrate is much stronger than brewed tea, and the caffeine reflects that. A grande Starbucks Chai Tea Latte has as much caffeine as a standard cup of brewed coffee.

Home-brewed chai lattes — made by steeping chai tea bags in warm milk — are significantly lower (roughly 40–60mg), because you're diluting weak brewed tea rather than a concentrate.

## How Does Each Affect Your Cut-Off Time?

Caffeine has a half-life of approximately 5 hours. A 95mg chai latte at 3pm means roughly 47mg is still in your system at 8pm — enough to affect sleep quality for sensitive individuals.

The same principles apply as for any caffeinated drink: what matters is the total mg consumed and when, relative to your bedtime.

## Making a Lower-Caffeine Chai Latte at Home

If you want the flavour of a chai latte without the coffee-level caffeine:

- Brew 1–2 chai tea bags in hot milk (or water + milk) for 3–4 minutes
- Use a rooibos chai blend for near-zero caffeine
- Avoid store-bought concentrates — they're where most of the caffeine is
- A DIY chai latte typically comes in around 40–50mg

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does a chai latte have as much caffeine as coffee?
At Starbucks, a grande Chai Tea Latte (95mg) is comparable to a regular brewed coffee (around 80–100mg for a standard 8oz cup). It's lower than a Starbucks Caffe Latte (150mg) but not a low-caffeine option.

### Why does a chai latte have so much caffeine?
Coffee shop chai lattes use a pre-made chai concentrate rather than brewed tea. The concentrate is strong and contributes significantly more caffeine than a standard steeped tea bag.

### Is a chai latte a good alternative to coffee for reducing caffeine?
A home-brewed chai latte (40–60mg) is a meaningful step down from coffee. A coffee shop chai latte (70–95mg) is roughly equivalent to a regular coffee, so it's not a reliable lower-caffeine substitute.

### How much caffeine is in an iced chai latte from Starbucks?
A Starbucks Iced Chai Tea Latte (grande, 16oz) contains approximately 95mg of caffeine — the same as the hot version.

### Can I make a caffeine-free chai latte?
Yes. Use a rooibos chai blend (rooibos is naturally caffeine-free) and steep in warm milk. You get the full spiced chai flavour with zero caffeine.
