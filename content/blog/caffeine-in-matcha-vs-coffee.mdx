## Quick Answer

**A standard matcha drink (8oz) contains 40–70mg of caffeine. A brewed coffee contains 80–100mg.** Coffee has more caffeine — but matcha's is delivered differently, which is why the energy feels cleaner to many people.

---

## Why Matcha Caffeine Feels Different

Both matcha and coffee deliver caffeine, but matcha contains L-theanine — an amino acid that promotes calm focus and blunts the spike-and-crash effect that coffee can cause. The combination of caffeine and L-theanine is why matcha is often described as giving "alert calm" rather than jittery energy.

The caffeine content of matcha also depends heavily on quality and preparation:

- **Ceremonial grade matcha** — highest quality, used in traditional preparation (whisked with water). 60–70mg per 8oz.
- **Culinary grade matcha** — used in lattes and baking. Lower caffeine, 40–60mg per 8oz.
- **Matcha latte (coffee shop)** — depends on how many scoops, typically 40–80mg.

| Green tea | 20–45mg |

Matcha is considerably stronger than regular green tea — this surprises many people. Because you consume the whole ground leaf (rather than steeping and discarding), you ingest more caffeine than a standard cup of green tea.

## Does Matcha Affect Your Sleep?

Yes. At 40–70mg per serving, matcha has a similar effect on sleep timing as a moderate coffee. The caffeine half-life of 5 hours applies regardless of the source — whether it came from coffee beans or green tea leaves.

The fact that matcha feels gentler doesn't mean it clears your system faster. 60mg of caffeine from matcha is still 60mg of caffeine.

## Which Is Better for Energy — Matcha or Coffee?

This depends on what you want:

- **Need a fast, strong hit?** Coffee's faster-rising caffeine spike wins for immediate alertness.
- **Want sustained focus without jitters?** Matcha's L-theanine combination is better for calm, steady concentration.
- **Sensitive to caffeine anxiety?** Matcha is often better tolerated.
- **Watching caffeine intake?** Matcha gives you a meaningful dose but typically less than coffee.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does matcha have more caffeine than coffee?
No. Brewed coffee contains 80–100mg per 8oz compared to 40–70mg for a typical matcha preparation. Coffee has roughly 30–60% more caffeine.

### Why does matcha feel different to coffee even though it has caffeine?
Matcha contains L-theanine, an amino acid that modulates the effects of caffeine — promoting calm focus and reducing the anxiety and jitteriness some people experience with coffee.

### How much caffeine is in a Starbucks Matcha Latte?
A Starbucks Matcha Tea Latte (grande, 16oz) contains approximately 80mg of caffeine — comparable to a standard cup of brewed coffee.

### Is matcha safe to drink every day?
For most healthy adults, yes. At 40–70mg per serving, one or two daily matcha drinks keeps you well within the 400mg recommended daily limit. Be mindful of timing — matcha has the same 5-hour caffeine half-life as any other source.
