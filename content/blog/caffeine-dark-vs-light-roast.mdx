## Quick Answer

**Light roast coffee has slightly more caffeine than dark roast by weight.** Dark roast has a tiny edge if measured by volume (scoops). In practice, the difference is negligible — around 5–10mg per cup.

---

## Why Most People Get This Wrong

The intuition is that dark roast = stronger = more caffeine. It's wrong.

Roasting destroys a small amount of caffeine. The longer and hotter the roast, the more caffeine is lost. So by weight, light roast is marginally more caffeinated than dark roast.

The confusion arises from the measurement method:

- **By weight (grams):** Light roast wins — denser beans, more caffeine per gram.
- **By volume (scoops):** Dark roast may edge ahead — roasting expands the bean, making it larger and less dense. A scoop of dark roast contains more beans than a scoop of light roast.

Most home coffee makers use scoops, not scales. So in practice, the difference is small either way — typically under 10mg per cup.

## Dark Roast vs Light Roast: Caffeine Comparison

| Coffee type | Caffeine per 8oz (approx) |
|---|---|
| Light roast (drip, by weight) | 85–100mg |
| Medium roast (drip, by weight) | 80–95mg |
| Dark roast (drip, by weight) | 75–90mg |
| Espresso (dark roast, 1oz shot) | 63mg |
| Espresso (light roast, 1oz shot) | 60–70mg |

The differences are genuinely small. Both light and dark roast coffee deliver broadly similar caffeine — the roast level is not a meaningful lever for controlling caffeine intake.

## What Actually Determines Caffeine in Your Coffee?

Far more important than roast level:

1. **Coffee-to-water ratio** — more coffee, more caffeine. The single biggest variable.
2. **Brew method** — cold brew (100–200mg/8oz) vs drip (80–100mg) vs espresso shot (63mg/1oz).
3. **Grind size** — finer grinds have greater surface area and extract more caffeine.
4. **Brew time** — longer contact extracts more caffeine (most relevant for French press).
5. **Bean variety** — Robusta has roughly double the caffeine of Arabica. Most specialty coffee is Arabica.

## Does Roast Level Affect How Coffee Makes You Feel?

Yes, though not through caffeine. Dark roast contains slightly less acid, which some people find easier on the stomach. Light roast retains more of the bean's original flavour compounds (fruit, brightness, floral notes). These are flavour and stomach comfort differences — not energy differences.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does dark roast have more caffeine than light roast?
No. The common assumption is wrong. Light roast has marginally more caffeine by weight because roasting destroys a small amount of caffeine. When measured by volume (scoops), dark roast may edge ahead due to less-dense beans, but the difference is typically under 10mg.

### Why does dark roast taste stronger if it has less caffeine?
"Strong" in taste and "high in caffeine" are not the same thing. Dark roast has a more intense, bitter flavour profile because roasting produces more of the compounds responsible for coffee's roasty taste. Caffeine is relatively tasteless — the bitterness you associate with strength comes from other compounds.

### Which coffee has the most caffeine?
Cold brew has the most caffeine of any common coffee drink — 100–200mg per 8oz. Among hot coffees, drip coffee from Robusta beans (common in some instant coffees) is highest. Standard Arabica drip coffee runs 80–100mg regardless of roast.

### Does espresso have more caffeine than filter coffee?
Per ounce, espresso is more concentrated (63mg in 1oz vs roughly 10–12mg per oz of drip coffee). But a standard espresso shot is just 1oz, so the total caffeine per serving is actually lower than a full 8oz cup of drip coffee.

### Does the coffee brand matter for caffeine?
Yes. Robusta-based coffees (common in some supermarket blends and instant coffee) have roughly double the caffeine of Arabica. If you're buying specialty or single-origin coffee, it's almost certainly Arabica. Budget supermarket blends may contain Robusta.
