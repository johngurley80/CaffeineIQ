## Quick Answer

**Brewed chai tea contains approximately 40–70mg of caffeine per 8oz cup** — similar to standard black tea. A chai tea latte from a coffee shop typically contains 70–95mg due to concentrated syrups.

---

## Why Chai Tea Caffeine Varies So Much

"Chai tea" covers a wide range of products:

- **Loose leaf masala chai** — brewed from black tea + spices. Caffeine entirely from the tea.
- **Chai tea bags** — convenient, but caffeine varies by brand (40–70mg typical).
- **Chai concentrate / syrup** — used in coffee shops. Often has added sugar and higher caffeine.
- **Chai tea latte** — concentrate + steamed milk. Caffeine depends on concentrate ratio.
- **Powdered chai mixes** — lowest caffeine, sometimes 25–40mg per serving.

The spices in chai (cardamom, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, black pepper) contribute zero caffeine. All caffeine comes from the black tea base.

## Chai Tea vs Coffee: Which Has More Caffeine?

A standard cup of brewed coffee (80–100mg) contains more caffeine than most brewed chai teas (40–70mg). However, a Starbucks Chai Tea Latte at 95mg is broadly comparable to a standard coffee.

If you're switching from coffee to chai thinking you're reducing caffeine intake — you probably are, but a latte from a coffee shop may not give you the reduction you expect.

## Does Chai Tea Affect Sleep?

At 40–70mg per cup, chai tea has a meaningful effect on sleep if consumed within 6–8 hours of bedtime for most people. Caffeine has a half-life of around 5 hours, meaning half the caffeine from a 5pm cup of chai is still in your system at 10pm.

If you're having chai as an evening wind-down drink, consider:
- Choosing an herbal chai (rooibos-based, no black tea)
- Brewing for less time
- Checking your specific cut-off based on your weight and bedtime

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does chai tea have caffeine?
Yes. Most chai tea contains caffeine from its black tea base — typically 40–70mg per 8oz brewed cup. Herbal chai blends made with rooibos or other caffeine-free bases are the exception.

### How much caffeine is in a Starbucks Chai Tea Latte?
A Starbucks Chai Tea Latte (grande, 16oz) contains 95mg of caffeine. A tall (12oz) contains 70mg. This is higher than most home-brewed chai because Starbucks uses a concentrated chai syrup.

### Is chai tea high in caffeine?
Compared to coffee (80–100mg), brewed chai tea is moderate at 40–70mg. However, chai lattes from coffee shops can match or exceed regular coffee depending on concentrate strength.

### Can I drink chai tea before bed?
It depends on your caffeine sensitivity. Brewed chai tea at 40–70mg will affect most people's sleep if consumed within 5–6 hours of bedtime. Herbal chai blends (rooibos-based) contain no caffeine and are genuinely sleep-safe.

### Which has more caffeine — chai tea or coffee?
Brewed coffee typically has more: 80–100mg vs 40–70mg for brewed chai. A chai latte from a coffee shop (95mg) is roughly equivalent to a standard coffee.
